Start Here: Citizen Guide to the Overtime Helper

This tool helps workers estimate weekly overtime under Motor Vehicle Operator Hours of Work rules and ask follow-up questions with a local AI assistant.

Who is this for?

  • Bus operators checking if weekly hours exceed standard thresholds.
  • City and highway operators comparing schedules to overtime rules.
  • Workers with mixed duties preparing for a pay conversation with an employer.
  • Citizens preparing documents for a labour standards inquiry.

What this app can do

  1. Quick Estimate — Enter weekly totals by work type and get an instant overtime and pay estimate.
  2. Daily Breakdown — Enter hours for each day of the week. The tool calculates both daily and weekly overtime, then uses whichever is higher (better for you), exactly as the MVOHWR requires.
  3. Save & Track — Save weekly estimates to your browser history so you can build evidence over time. Print or save as PDF for your records.
  4. Know Your Rights — Step-by-step guidance on what to do if you think you are owed overtime, with direct links to the Federal Labour Program.
  5. Chat Assistant — Ask follow-up questions in plain language using the built-in Ollama AI assistant.
  6. Bilingual — Toggle between English and French at any time.

How to use (citizen flow)

  1. Open the calculator page.
  2. Choose Quick Estimate for weekly totals, or Daily Breakdown for a 7-day grid.
  3. Enter your hours by work type (bus / city / highway / other) and your hourly wage.
  4. Click Estimate pay or Calculate daily + weekly OT.
  5. Review the results. For daily breakdown, a table shows each day's overtime.
  6. Click Save this week to store the result in your browser history.
  7. Use Print / Save PDF to create a document for your employer or a labour complaint.
  8. Check the Know Your Rights tab for next steps if you believe you are owed money.
  9. Use the chat section to ask clarifying questions based on your estimate.

Example use cases for citizens

  • "I worked 52 city hours; how many should be paid at overtime rate?"
  • "My paystub seems low; can I compare expected overtime with paid hours?"
  • "I split city and highway duties this week; what threshold likely applies?"
  • "I want to track my hours week by week to build evidence for a complaint."
  • "What evidence should I collect before filing a complaint?"

Important limits

This is an informational estimate, not legal advice. Final entitlement depends on complete employment details, contracts, and official interpretation. When in doubt, contact the Labour Program at 1-800-641-4049.
